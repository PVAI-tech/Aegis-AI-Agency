Accessibility Statement

Last updated: 18 July 2026

We want this site to be usable by as many people as possible, including people using assistive technology. This page is an honest account of where things stand, not a claim of full compliance.

What's in place

  • Every interactive modal (service details, the free-audit prompt) traps focus while open, closes on Esc or an outside click, and returns focus to whatever triggered it on close.
  • Service cards and other custom interactive elements are keyboard-operable (Tab to focus, Enter/Space to activate), not just clickable with a mouse.
  • The Jarvis chat widget and modals carry appropriate ARIA attributes (aria-haspopup, aria-expanded, aria-modal, aria-label) for screen readers.
  • Text contrast has been manually checked against the site's color palette: body text passes WCAG AA comfortably; the brand accent color is used only for buttons, icons, and large text, never small body copy, specifically because it doesn't pass AA contrast at small sizes.
  • All images carry descriptive alt text.

Known limitations

  • No formal automated accessibility audit (e.g. axe-core or a full Lighthouse accessibility run) has been carried out against the live site yet.
  • The site has not been tested with a screen reader end-to-end by someone who uses one daily — the ARIA work above is based on standard practice, not first-hand assistive-technology testing.

Reporting an issue

If you hit an accessibility barrier anywhere on this site, tell us — email or WhatsApp both work, and we'll treat it as a real bug to fix, not a formality.
